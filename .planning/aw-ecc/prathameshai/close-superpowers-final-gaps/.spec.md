# Spec: Close Remaining Superpowers Parity Gaps

## Goal

Close the remaining meaningful parity gaps between AW SDLC and `obra/superpowers` without expanding the AW public command surface.

## Scope

The remaining gaps to close are:

1. always-on activation strictness
2. operational worktree automation
3. true worker-runtime depth for `aw-execute`

## Constraints

- Keep the public AW surface unchanged:
  - `/aw:plan`
  - `/aw:execute`
  - `/aw:verify`
  - `/aw:deploy`
  - `/aw:ship`
- Keep internal helpers internal.
- Prefer repo-local routing and runtime behavior over global fallback behavior.
- Keep deterministic artifacts and governance rules intact.

## Acceptance Criteria

### Activation strictness

- Repo-local startup instructions clearly require AW route selection before any substantive response.
- Session-start routing context reinforces that strict activation rule.
- Deterministic evals assert the stricter startup behavior.

### Worktree automation

- `aw-prepare` grows from classify/recommend to an operational lifecycle contract.
- The repo ships a concrete helper script for worktree lifecycle operations.
- `aw-finish` and `aw-ship` understand prepare-created workspace metadata and cleanup rules.
- Deterministic evals assert the lifecycle contract and the helper’s presence.

### Worker runtime depth

- `aw-execute` references concrete internal worker runtime assets, not just role descriptions.
- The repo ships reusable worker templates or manifests for implementer/spec-review/quality-review flows.
- The repo ships a helper script that can materialize worker bundles from approved task inputs.
- Deterministic evals assert the worker-runtime packaging and dispatch contract.

## Non-Goals

- Do not add new public AW commands.
- Do not require live external side effects for deterministic tests.
- Do not depend on harness-specific subagent features for correctness.
